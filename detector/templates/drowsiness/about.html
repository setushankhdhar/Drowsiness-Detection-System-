About the Project

Preventing Fatigue
One Blink at a Time

Drowsiness Detection System is a computer-vision safety system that detects driver drowsiness in real time using facial landmark analysis — running entirely on your local machine, no cloud required.

Core Algorithm

Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR)

The system uses the Eye Aspect Ratio — a ratio of vertical to horizontal eye distances computed from 6 facial landmarks per eye.

When eyes are open, EAR stays around 0.25–0.30. When closed, it drops sharply toward zero. If EAR stays below the threshold for multiple consecutive frames, a drowsiness alert fires.

The formula: EAR = (‖p2−p6‖ + ‖p3−p5‖) / (2 × ‖p1−p4‖)

Eye Aspect Ratio — visual

≥ 0.25 Alert
0.15–0.25 Warning
< 0.15 Drowsy

Limitations

Known Constraints

💡

Lighting sensitivity

Very dark or overexposed environments can reduce landmark detection accuracy.

👓

Glasses & occlusion

Thick frames or tinted lenses may interfere with EAR calculation.

📐

Camera angle

Works best with the camera directly facing the driver at eye level.

⚙️

Threshold tuning

Default EAR thresholds may need adjustment for different face shapes.
